# LinkedIn Recruiter Alternatives: How to Build a Modern Sourcing Stack Without a Huge Seat Cost

LinkedIn Recruiter is still useful. It is also expensive, crowded, and often incomplete for hard technical searches.

The real question is not “what single tool replaces LinkedIn Recruiter?” The better question is: what jobs does LinkedIn Recruiter do, and which tools can replace each job?

## The four jobs LinkedIn Recruiter does

1. Search the LinkedIn database
2. Organize projects and profiles
3. Send InMail
4. Provide some market visibility

A modern sourcing stack can replace parts of that workflow with a mix of tools.

## The free or lower-cost stack

For technical roles:
- Google X-Ray
- GitHub
- Stack Overflow
- OpenAlex for researchers
- Hugging Face for AI/ML contributors
- BooleanOS for strings
- SourcingOS for project logic and evidence tracking

For contact discovery:
- ContactOut
- Apollo
- Lusha
- RocketReach
- Hunter
- Clearbit-style enrichment where appropriate

For GovCon:
- ClearanceJobs where available
- GovCon donor-company maps
- Google X-Ray
- ATS rediscovery
- SourcingOS source packs

For healthcare:
- NPI Registry
- state licensing boards where allowed
- hospital systems
- association pages
- specialized job boards

## When LinkedIn Recruiter still wins

LinkedIn still wins when:

- The target market is active on LinkedIn
- InMail is part of the workflow
- Hiring managers expect LinkedIn profile review
- Your team already has clean project workflows there
- The search depends heavily on current title/company filters

## Where alternatives win

Alternatives win when:

- The talent is open-source-heavy
- The market is technical and underrepresented on LinkedIn
- Contact data matters more than InMail
- The role requires public evidence
- You need to compare multiple data sources
- You are priced out of enterprise licenses

## Example stack under a small budget

A practical small-team stack might look like:

- SourcingOS / BooleanOS for search strategy and strings
- Google X-Ray for open-web discovery
- GitHub for technical evidence
- ContactOut or Apollo for contact enrichment
- Google Sheets or Airtable for early tracking
- Gmail or manual outreach for first campaigns

That will not replace every LinkedIn Recruiter feature, but it can cover a surprising amount of sourcing work if the recruiter has a clear methodology.
